Navigating the Bull:

A Strategic Approach to Cryptocurrency Trading

Within the cryptocurrency markets, one should stick to a well planned trading strategy. Thanks to the insights gained from @karasurrect's private telegram group, I've honed in on a strategy that not only minimizes risk but also enhances the potential for substantial rewards.

In the volatile realm of crypto, risk management is a must. Opting for spot trading over leverage trading has proven to be a wise decision in protecting funds, avoiding the posibility of liquidation. Learning this lesson, having liquidated more crypto than some earn in a year, I realized the importance of spot trading to build a solid foundation of understanding the markets.

My investment philosophy revolves around trading coins or tokens that could be held with a long-term outlook. Today, I digress from shilling specific cryptocurrencies, keeping you in suspence for a future blog post.

My focus is on mid-cap cryptocurrencies that tend to make higher and lower price swings, offering a balance of risk and reward. These trades and investments are kept to cryptocurrencies ranked between 100-450 on coinmarketcap.com. I prioritize trading cryptocurrencies listed on major exchanges particularly Binance and Coinbase due to their substantial liquidity.

Timing is crucial and difficult to master while trading. I advocate for buying after a market crash due to news or a stagnent market, but with caution. Waiting for a stabilization period of 1-3 days after bearish trends turn bullish to ensure a more informed entry point. Implementing a dollar-cost averaging buy approach throughout the day after the candles stop hitting lower lows further mitigates the impact of market volatility.

It would be clever to use and adjust a stop-loss, trailing them just below resistance as the asset's price climbs. This safeguards profits and ensures that profits are locked in as the market fluctuates.

The realization of profits is a scaled process. After surpassing each resistance level, a percentage of the holding is systematically sold. My initial approach involves selling 25% of the holdings at each level, providing a structured method to secure gains while leaving room for more potential upward returns.

In addition, allocating a portion of profits to a self-custody wallet, with the intention of preserving and building a long-term HODL savings plan, adds a crucial layer of stability and foresight to one's cryptocurrency portfolio.

In conclusion, navigating the bullish market requires a well planned strategy to maximize your gains. By incorporating risk management, spot trading, and strategic entry and exit points, one can navigate the cryptocurrency market with confidence.
